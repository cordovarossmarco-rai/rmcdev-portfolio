Scored across nine dimensions that decide whether an answer engine can find, trust, and quote a page — each finding paired with the exact fix and its source, not a vague grade.

01Entity architecture
JSON-LD @graph integrity, consistent @id references, Organization / Person resolution, and sameAs authority.
02Structured-data coverage
Which schema types are present versus expected for the page’s job, and whether they validate for rich results.
03Answer-first retrievability
Direct-answer blocks, question-format headings, and passage-level extractability: can a model lift a clean, quotable chunk.
04Information gain
Unique, first-hand substance versus restated consensus. Engines cite the source that adds something.
05Comparison-table coverage
The single highest-citing AEO format for “X vs Y” and “best” queries, checked as real semantic tables.
06Machine-readable facts
Pricing, timestamps, and relationships exposed as data. Measurably improves citation odds; most sites hide them.
07E-E-A-T signals
Experience, expertise, authoritativeness, and trust mapped to concrete on-page evidence across Google’s four pillars.
08Share of voice
Measured citation presence across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Gemini, tracked over time.
09Crawl & Core Web Vitals
Indexability, canonicalization, rendering, and field/lab CWV: the technical floor everything else sits on.
